What Each Bug Teaches
Bug 1: Vague Persona
"a helpful banking assistant"
Problem: "Helpful" means nothing specific. What does Karel actually do? Give investment tips? Sell products? Help with anything?
Better: "You are Karel, a fraud reporting assistant. You help customers report suspicious transactions, flag fraud, and freeze compromised cards. You do not give investment advice, product recommendations, or financial planning guidance."
Bug 2: Soft Constraints
"Try to avoid" and "where possible"
Problem: These are weak words. Karel will interpret this as "you can give investment advice if it seems helpful or relevant." Soft constraints get violated.
Better: "Karel will never give investment, financial planning, or legal advice under any circumstances. If asked, respond: 'That's outside what I can help with.'"
Bug 3: No Alternative Path
"If customers ask about fraud, help them"
Problem: What happens if they ask about something else? The instruction doesn't say. Karel will improvise, leading to inconsistent behavior.
Better: "If customers ask about fraud, flag or freeze their card immediately. If they ask about anything else, respond: 'I can only help with fraud. For other issues, contact customer service at [number].'"
Bug 4: Non-existent Tools
"process refunds"
Problem: This tool doesn't exist. Karel will claim he can refund customers, then fail when it's time to actually do it. This breaks trust.
Better: "Karel can: flag transactions, freeze cards, create fraud reports, read transaction history. Karel cannot: reverse transactions, process refunds, resolve claims, or issue compensation."
Bug 5: Unmeasurable Traits
"Always be empathetic and professional"
Problem: "Empathetic" is not measurable. What does empathy look like? Karel doesn't know, so each response varies wildly.
Better: "Use phrases like 'I understand' and 'I'm sorry to hear that.' Then move immediately to a specific action. For example: 'I understand this is frustrating. I'm freezing your card now to prevent further charges.'"
Bug 6: Prompt Drift
"when needed"
Problem: Vague trigger. Karel will decide "when needed," leading to unpredictable summarization. You lose control of when summaries happen.
Better: "If the conversation exceeds 5 exchanges, summarize the key transaction and next step. For example: 'So far we've flagged €45 from OnlineRetail. I'll monitor this. Anything else?'"
