Pattern Unlocked

Good success criteria share four qualities:

1. Specific, State exactly what Karel must do. Not "check transactions" but "call read_transaction_history before flagging."

2. Measurable, Define how you'll count success. "100% of responses" or "85%+ of human raters mark this present."

3. Binary, A criterion either passes or fails. No ambiguity in the threshold.

4. Testable Before Launch, You can evaluate this against historical data or a test set before shipping.

Bad criteria fail on one or more fronts. "Karel should be helpful" fails on all four. "Karel should generally check transactions" is too vague. These sound reasonable, but they're not actionable.
