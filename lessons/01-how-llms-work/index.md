# Module 1: How LLMs Actually Work

Everything in generative AI, agents, hallucinations, prompting, memory, flows from one foundational idea: a language model is a system trained to predict what comes next in a sequence of text.

That sounds simple. The implications are enormous.

In this module, we'll build up a mental model of what an LLM is, how it learned what it knows, and why its architecture creates both remarkable capabilities and hard limits. By the end, you'll be able to reason about *why* an AI behaves a certain way, not just *that* it does.
