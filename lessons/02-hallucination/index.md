# Module 2: The Hallucination Problem

If Module 1 explained *how* LLMs work, this module explains the most important failure mode that flows directly from that mechanism: hallucination.

Hallucination is when an LLM confidently states something that is factually wrong. Not wrong because it misunderstood you, wrong because it generated a plausible-sounding but incorrect answer. It's the dark side of next-token prediction.

Understanding hallucination isn't just academic. It's one of the most important things an interviewer will probe when you're designing an AI system. Knowing what causes it, and how to reduce it, is what separates a thoughtful AI product designer from someone who just read a blog post about GPT.
